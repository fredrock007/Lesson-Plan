Topic

Excerpt “The Suit” by Can Themba

Lesson Objectives

  • Identify key story parts (like who, where, and what’s happening).
  • Understand how details help tell the story’s meaning.
  • Write answers using examples from the story.

⏱️ PDN (Please Do Now)

Sentence: "He stood in the doorway."

💡 Tip: Tap the colored parts to see what they mean or why they’re important!

📘 View Example

✖

Expanded Example: He stood in the doorway, shoulders tense beneath his worn jacket, the pale light from the corridor framing his silhouette like a ghost returning home. The air smelled faintly of damp and dust, whispering secrets from a time he’d tried to forget.

✖

🎨 This sentence helps you picture the man clearly. It shows he’s tense (worried or scared) and uses light to make him look mysterious — like something important is about to happen.

✖

👃 This sentence uses smell to help you imagine the place. “Damp and dust” makes the room feel old and full of memories.

❓ Do It

  1. Who is the main character and what does he want?
  2. Where and when does the story happen?
  3. What problem or fight happens in the story?

💡 Click the colored sentences in the answers to learn what makes them special!

📘 View Answers

✖
  • Main Character: Jabu wants to find out the truth about a suit and what it means to his wife, Mei, and his brother.
  • Setting: The story happens in a small flat (apartment) in South Africa, long ago. It feels closed in — like Jabu is trapped by his feelings.
  • Conflict: Jabu feels hurt and confused because he thinks Mei is keeping secrets about his brother. The suit reminds him of sadness and truth.
✖

🧠 This explains what drives Jabu — he wants answers and feels mixed emotions like anger and love.

✖

🏠 The place shows how small and heavy their world feels, matching their sad feelings.

✖

💔 The suit is like a memory that hurts — it keeps reminding Jabu of the bad things he can’t forget.

📖 Do It Again

Write your own paragraph showing a problem between two people.

💡 Click on the colors in the example to see how emotions are shown through words.

📘 View Example

✖

Example Response:
Jabu held the suit tightly, his hands shaking with anger and sadness. Mei looked away, saying nothing — her silence spoke louder than words. The air between them felt heavy, full of things they both wanted to say but couldn’t.

✖

😠 This shows Jabu’s feelings through his body, not by saying “he was angry.”

✖

🤫 Silence can be powerful — sometimes not speaking shows guilt or sadness.

✖

💬 The space around them feels like their emotions — full and tense, just like their hearts.

📝 Independent Practice

Write a short paragraph that shows how someone feels without saying it directly.

💡 Tap the colors in the example to learn how writers use details to show emotion.

📘 View Example

✖

Example Paragraph:
The room felt smaller than ever as Jabu’s questions filled the air. Mei stood by the window, her reflection mixing with the city lights. The suit hung in the corner — black, shiny, and still. Jabu saw not just a coat but a memory that made him sad all over again. The kettle started to whistle — the only sound brave enough to break the silence.

✖

🏠 This shows that feelings can make a room feel small and tight — like being trapped.

✖

🌃 The lights show she feels far away, lost in her own world.

✖

🧥 The suit isn’t just clothes — it’s a reminder of something sad.

✖

💭 Sometimes memories hurt more than what’s happening right now.

✖

☕ Even simple sounds, like the kettle, can make a moment feel real and calm.
